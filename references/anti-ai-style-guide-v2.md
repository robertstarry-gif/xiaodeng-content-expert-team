# Anti-AI Style Guide V2

Use when the user says the output has AI-feel, when writing for public readers, or when a draft sounds generic.

## The Real Test

Ask:

> Would a real person with this problem say "yes, that is the thing" in the first 10 seconds?

If not, the draft probably has an angle problem, not a vocabulary problem.

## Before Writing

Define four things:

1. **Scene**: where the reader is, what they are doing, what pressure they feel.
2. **Tension**: what choice, doubt, contradiction, or desire drives the piece.
3. **Specific material**: objects, actions, numbers, quotes, symptoms, examples, or steps.
4. **Boundary**: what the piece will not overclaim.

## Common AI-Feel Patterns

Remove or rewrite:

- "在当今快节奏的时代..."
- "随着 X 的发展..."
- "这不仅是 A，更是 B..."
- "真正重要的不是..."
- "让我们一起..."
- "总而言之，X 是一场旅程..."
- perfect three-part lists with no concrete example;
- balanced paragraphs that never choose;
- "温暖、有力量、治愈" without a scene;
- generic advice such as "保持沟通", "提升效率", "关注风险";
- motivational endings that could fit any article.

## Replacement Moves

Use:

- a concrete after-work moment;
- a reader's likely inner sentence;
- one specific example before explanation;
- a practical choice;
- a downside or exception;
- a line that closes the actual thought.

## Chinese Public Writing Checks

- Keep paragraphs short, but not chopped mechanically.
- Let some sentences be plain.
- Avoid overusing bold, emoji, and template labels.
- Do not explain every metaphor.
- Avoid "清单感" when the piece needs a story.
- Avoid "散文腔" when the piece needs a decision.

## Revision Questions

1. Which paragraph could be from any article on the internet?
2. Which line sounds polished but says nothing?
3. Where is the first concrete object or action?
4. What sentence carries the writer's actual judgment?
5. What would a reader copy, remember, or argue with?
