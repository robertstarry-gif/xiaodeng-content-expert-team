# Domain Risk Guide

Use this reference when the content domain changes the rules, especially for high-stakes topics.

## Risk Levels

- **Low**: creative, lifestyle, fiction, food storytelling, general brand copy with no factual or safety claims.
- **Medium**: product comparisons, marketing claims, statistics, career advice, relationship advice, nutrition claims, business strategy.
- **High**: investing, personal finance, law, medicine, health, safety, crisis, regulated products, compliance, claims about guaranteed outcomes.

High-risk topics can still be written, but the output must be educational, cautious, and explicit about limits.

## Food / Lifestyle

Check:

- ingredients and substitutions;
- allergies and dietary restrictions;
- cooking temperatures and food safety;
- cultural framing and naming;
- sensory detail that helps the reader imagine the result.

Avoid:

- unsafe preservation, raw-food, or fermentation advice without safety context;
- pretending a recipe has been tested if it has not.

## Relationships / Emotion

Check:

- whether the piece validates feelings without manipulating the reader;
- whether advice respects boundaries, consent, and safety;
- whether it avoids diagnosing people.

Avoid:

- coercive scripts;
- certainty about another person's inner state;
- advice that pressures someone to remain in unsafe situations.

## Finance / Investing

Use educational framing.

Do:

- distinguish general information from personal financial advice;
- explain assumptions, risks, time horizon, liquidity, and downside;
- verify current figures and laws when the claim depends on timing;
- avoid promising returns.

Do not:

- tell a specific person what to buy, sell, borrow, or invest in;
- imply guaranteed profit or risk-free returns;
- fabricate performance, backtests, credentials, or client outcomes.

## Legal

Use general information framing.

Do:

- identify jurisdiction when relevant;
- mark the date if law or regulation may change;
- suggest consulting a qualified lawyer for decisions;
- avoid drafting documents as final legal advice unless the user supplies counsel-approved language to adapt.

Do not:

- provide personalized legal strategy;
- state uncertain law as settled;
- ignore local jurisdiction.

## Medical / Health

Use educational framing.

Do:

- avoid diagnosis and treatment instructions for a specific person;
- encourage qualified clinicians for decisions;
- include urgent-care guidance when symptoms suggest immediate danger;
- verify current medical facts when tools and sources are available.

Do not:

- prescribe medication, dosage, or treatment;
- replace clinical judgment;
- present anecdote as proof.

## Fiction / Story

Check:

- protagonist desire;
- obstacle and stakes;
- scene-level specificity;
- voice consistency;
- pacing and emotional turn.

Avoid:

- theme lectures;
- exposition that replaces action;
- generic sensory detail.

## Brand Advertising / Marketing

Check:

- audience pain;
- differentiated promise;
- proof or credible reason to believe;
- objection handling;
- call to action;
- compliance and claim boundaries.

Avoid:

- deceptive scarcity;
- fake testimonials;
- unsupported superiority claims;
- health, finance, or legal promises disguised as marketing.
